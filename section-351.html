Section 351 Exchanges

Tax-free exchanges for ETF creation and redemption

What is Section 351?

Section 351 of the Internal Revenue Code provides for tax-free exchanges when property is transferred to a corporation in exchange for stock. For ETFs, this allows Authorized Participants (APs) to exchange baskets of securities for ETF shares without triggering immediate tax consequences.

This provision is fundamental to the ETF structure, enabling the in-kind creation and redemption process that makes ETFs tax-efficient investment vehicles.

Key Requirements

  • •
    Control Requirement: Transferors must own at least 80% of voting power and shares immediately after the exchange. This ensures the exchange qualifies as a tax-free transaction.
  • •
    Property for Stock: The exchange must involve securities solely for ETF shares. No cash or other property (boot) should be included in the exchange.
  • •
    No Boot: The exchange must be solely for stock to avoid gain recognition. Any boot received would trigger immediate tax consequences.

Tax Benefits

  • •
    Tax-Efficient Creation/Redemption: APs can exchange baskets of securities for ETF shares without triggering immediate taxable events, preserving capital gains deferral.
  • •
    In-Kind Redemptions: ETF shareholders can redeem shares in-kind, avoiding capital gains distributions that would occur in traditional mutual funds.
  • •
    Basis Preservation: The tax basis of the securities transferred carries over to the ETF shares received, maintaining the original cost basis for future tax calculations.

How It Works

Creation Process:

An AP delivers a basket of securities to the ETF in exchange for creation units (typically 50,000 shares). Under Section 351, this exchange is tax-free, allowing the AP to defer any capital gains.

Redemption Process:

An AP delivers creation units to the ETF in exchange for the underlying basket of securities. This in-kind redemption is also tax-free under Section 351.

Tax Disclaimer: This information is for educational purposes only and does not constitute tax, legal, or investment advice. The tax treatment of Section 351 exchanges can be complex and depends on specific facts and circumstances. Consult with a qualified tax advisor regarding your specific situation and the potential tax implications of ETF creation and redemption transactions.

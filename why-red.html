The innovation factor represents a persistent market inefficiency, similar to how other factor strategies exploit systematic mispricing. Companies with superior innovation metrics are systematically undervalued because:

  • → Short-termism causes markets to discount long-term innovation value

WHY THIS ANOMALY PERSISTS

Three structural forces create persistent mispricing that cannot be arbitraged away

📊

ACCOUNTING DISTORTION

R&D is expensed immediately, not capitalized. This creates a systematic accounting bias that understates innovation companies' true profitability and asset base. The distortion is structural—it's built into GAAP accounting rules.

Impact: -15% to -25% on reported ROE
🎲

UNCERTAINTY DISCOUNT

Innovation outcomes are inherently uncertain. Markets apply excessive discount rates to innovation investments, creating persistent mispricing. This isn't irrational—it's behavioral. Uncertainty creates opportunity.

Discount: 2-3x higher than warranted
⏱️

SHORT-TERM BIAS

Institutional investors focus on quarterly earnings. Innovation value compounds over years, not quarters, creating a systematic valuation gap. The market's time horizon is misaligned with innovation's value creation cycle.

Time Lag: 3-5 years to full value

Investment Objective

Combine quantitative factor screening with fundamental analysis to build a concentrated portfolio of high-conviction global equities exhibiting favorable value, quality, and momentum characteristics.

The fund uses a two-stage process: a systematic quantitative screen narrows a global universe to roughly 150 candidates based on composite factor scores, then fundamental analysis — focused on business quality, capital allocation discipline, and management credibility — selects the final 45 holdings. Weights reflect conviction, not market cap.

Why This Exists

The quant-vs-fundamental debate is a false dichotomy. Pure quant strategies suffer in regime changes because they don't understand why patterns exist. Pure fundamental strategies suffer from narrative bias and overconfidence. The intersection is where the edge lives. 55 bps to own it.

Additional Risk Disclosures

Concentration Risk: The Fund holds only 45 securities, which increases concentration risk relative to more diversified strategies. Poor performance by a single holding may have an outsized impact on overall returns.

Blended Approach Risk: The Fund's two-stage process (quantitative screening followed by fundamental analysis) may underperform strategies that rely exclusively on one approach. The fundamental overlay may override quantitative signals that would have been profitable, and vice versa.

Active Management Risk: The Fund is actively managed and may underperform passive strategies or benchmarks. The Fund's blended quant-fundamental process may not successfully identify securities with favorable risk-return characteristics.

Factor Exposure Risk: The Fund's systematic screen targets value, quality, and momentum factors. These factors may individually or collectively underperform in certain market regimes, and historical factor premia may not persist in the future.

Style Drift Risk: The fundamental overlay component of the investment process may introduce biases that cause the portfolio to deviate from the factor exposures targeted by the systematic screen. This may result in unintended risk exposures.

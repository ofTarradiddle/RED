Diamond World's Largest 111 ETF

Here's something that bothers me. The top 10 stocks are now over 20% of global market cap. That number was 9% in 1980. Everyone nods along and says "yeah, concentration," then goes back to owning 3,000 stocks like it's 1992. I don't get it. If you think there's even a reasonable chance that a few firms end up dominating the global economy — and the last twenty years of data say you should at least take the possibility seriously — then spreading your money across thousands of companies, most of which contribute essentially nothing to your returns, is a strange way to express that view. DAM1 owns the 111 largest companies on earth by market cap. That's it. Pro rata weights, rebalanced periodically, 11.1 bps. No magic, no secret sauce. If concentration keeps going, you own the beneficiaries. If it reverses, you still own the biggest, most liquid names in every major market. I can live with that.

Investment Objective

Own the 111 largest public companies in the world by market cap. Weight them pro rata. Rebalance. Repeat. Global universe — we don't care where they're listed.

Standard liquidity and eligibility screens apply. Nothing fancy. The whole point is that you shouldn't need to be clever about this — the market is telling you which companies matter most. We just listen.

Why This Exists

Most people's portfolios implicitly bet against concentration getting worse. They hold thousands of stocks, which is great if returns stay dispersed. Not great if they don't. DAM1 is the other side of that bet. 11.1 bps.

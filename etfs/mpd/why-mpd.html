The Investment Case for MPD

Why owning forty of the world's best compounding machines for 45 basis points might be the most patient thing in your portfolio.

The Compounding Illusion

Everyone talks about compounding. Buffett quotes. The penny-doubling thought experiment. But most companies don't actually compound. They grow, they peak, they regress to the mean. The S&P 500 turns over constantly — the companies that were compounding ten years ago are frequently not the ones compounding today.

Most investors who think they own compounders actually own companies in the late innings of a growth phase. Identifying the difference requires looking at the engine, not the scoreboard.

The Vital Signs

We screen for four vital signs.

First

Persistent returns on invested capital above the cost of capital — not one good year, not three, but a sustained pattern that suggests structural advantage.

Second

Reinvestment runways that aren't shrinking — a company with 30% ROIC but nowhere to deploy capital is a dividend stock, not a compounder.

Third

Low capital intensity — the best compounders generate free cash flow that's disproportionate to their asset base.

Fourth

Insider alignment — management that allocates capital like owners, not like hired agents optimizing for quarterly earnings.

Why Management Matters More Than You Think

Quantitative investors usually dismiss management quality as unmeasurable. We disagree. Capital allocation is a decision trail. You can see it in the numbers: acquisition track records, buyback timing, reinvestment rates, compensation structures.

A CEO who buys back stock at 30x earnings and issues it at 15x is telling you something. We listen.

The Portfolio

MPD owns forty companies. That's concentrated. Deliberately so. We don't want the 200th-best compounder diluting the returns of the 10th-best. Every name in the portfolio has to pass all four vital signs.

Many well-known quality names don't make the cut — they fail on reinvestment runway, or their ROIC is declining, or management is extracting value rather than creating it. Forty names. No filler.

The Risk You're Taking

Quality compounders can underperform in speculative rallies. When junk rallies, this portfolio won't keep up. That's the trade-off. You're betting that over a full cycle, owning businesses with durable economics and skilled operators beats owning whatever is exciting this quarter.

History supports the bet. Patience is required. 45 bps.
